Where's Your Flash Drive?

December 19, 2016
Jamie Harvey

We live in a Golden Age of storage.  It has never been easier or cheaper to store large amounts of data.  This article focuses on USB Flash Drives, and why you probably don't have enough of them.

I recently overheard a conversation that went a little like this. 
    Person A: I need to save these files but they're too big to e-mail to myself.
    Person B: Can you save them to DropBox or Google Drive?
    Person A: I don't want to make an account/I can't remember my password.
    Person B: Well can't you just make a new account?
    Person A: I tried and it says an account is already registered to my e-mail.  Isn't there an easier way?

At this point in the conversation, I had to interject.  There was an easier way.  In fact there were multiple easier ways, and I'll be focusing on two of them.

At the time of this posting, there are at least ten different 32 GB flash drives that can be had for under $10.  Any one of these flash drives is smaller than one of your fingers, and holds an amount of data that just 20 years ago would have required a server the size of an entire room.  They can be attached to your keychain, put in a briefcase, or even stuck in that one tiny pocket of your jeans that isn't big enough for anything else.  Each of these flash drives has an option for encryption, so even if you lose possession of it your data isn't flowing freely.  It's almost 2017, and at this point there is absolutely no reason you shouldn't have a small flash drive to save your data and move it around.

"But wait," you're saying.  "You said there were 2 easier ways."  You are absolutely correct reader!  If for some reason you didn't want to plop down a crisp Hamilton for a USB flash drive, there's still a pretty good chance that you're walking around with storage in your pocket.  If you own a smartphone (and if you don't then honestly it's kind of weird that you're reading my blog) then you have storage!  If you have an Android device, plug it into whatever computer holds your data that you want, and from the phone menu choose the MTP file transfer protocol.  Then inside of the computers file explorer you will see your phone's file system.  Feel free to drag and drop to your heart's content.

If you have an iPhone, the procedure is a bit more complicated.  Connect your iPhone to your computer and open iTunes.  Select your phone and click Apps -> File Sharing -> Flash Drive.  Then you can click on "Add Files..." and add the files you want to your iPhone.  Then, next time you get a chance, ditch the iPhone and buy an Android.

So if you have no idea what to ask Santa for, you can never go wrong with a SanDisk Ultra Fit 128GB USB 3.0 Flash Drive (SDCZ43-128G-GAM46) [Newest Version]
