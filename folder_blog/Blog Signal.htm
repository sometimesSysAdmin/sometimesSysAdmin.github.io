Signal Messaging

December 21, 2016
Jamie Harvey

While listening to the Security Now podcast, I learned of Signal.  Similar to WhatsApp, this messaging service supports encrypted messaging.  But unlike WhatsApp, Signal takes their encryption to the next level.  This post explores the necessity of encryption and why you should start using Signal YESTERDAY!

With the wave of terrorist attacks still fresh in everyone's minds, the authorities have a great deal of investigating ahead of them.  As seen in the San Bernadino attack, much of this investigating involves searching the (alleged) attacker's messages.  Indeed, a large portion of media coverage involved a recovered iPhone that belonged to the attacker.  The FBI attempted to force Apple to unlock this phone, with Apple denying that it had this capability.  Of course, it is the duty of the authorities to explore and investigate every piece of information available to them.  While Apple avoided its hand being forced (a 3rd party was able to unlock it) the precedent that could be set has huge implications on the few privacies Americans have left.  Many believe the government should have the ability to decrypt these messages, and I'm here to tell you why that's not the case.

The Founding Fathers could never have imagined the invention of computers, much less how heavily every day life relies on them.  If you tell me you can make it through everyday life without the use of a single computer, I will happily call you a liar and question how you're reading this blog post.  But one thing the Founding Fathers did imagine is that the government would take every chance it could to infringe on the rights of the people.  They knew because they lived it.
