2-Factor Authentication

June 11, 2017
Jamie Harvey

I recently took a survey of people in an Information Assurance class regarding their use of multi factor authentication (MFA).  The results completely surprised me.  I was the only one using ANY form of MFA on my school email account.  Our school email accounts are sitting in Google's cloud, and Google makes MFA so easy that I couldn't believe this.  I was ready for the debate on why time based token authentication is much more secure than email, SMS, or calls and instead I received silence.  If this is how a Computer Science student in an Information Assurance course secures their email, what does that say about the every day users who are far less security conscious?  Perhaps at some point, mandatory MFA will have to be implemented to force users to secure their accounts. In the trade-off of security and convenience, opening the Google Authenticator app to punch in a 6 digit code is such a small inconvenience that exponentially increases security.
