About Myself

After graduating high school, I went to college at UNLV for a Pre-Professional Biology degree. However, after 5 years and deciding to double-major in Biology and Physics I decided that I needed a change. Spending 17 years in school and looking at even more for Medical School left me feeling like I was missing out on life.

After graduation at UNLV, I decided to enlist in the US Army as an Infantryman. While some people frown upon this job choice, I felt it allowed me to become a "jack of all trades." Almost every single role in a typical Infantry Company is filled by Infantryman, and there is a lot of cross-training. I got to do some very cool things (jump out of airplanes) and attend some very cool schools (Special Forces Assessment and Selection, Special Operations Combat Medic Course, Machine Gun Leaders Course, etc). After being injured during nighttime airborne operations, I was assigned as the Company Armorer while awaiting a medical discharge. I was in charge of over $6M in cutting edge military equipment. On January 1st, 2015, I received my Honorable Discharge, and moved on to the next chapter in my life.

While awaiting discharge, my girlfriend and I had the greatest blessing in my life and learned that she was pregnant. We got married shortly after, and on March 20th, 2015 she gave birth to a beautiful baby boy. With the goal of finding a career that I loved and providing for my family, I began pursuing a Computer Science degree back at UNLV. I soon found a position as a student worker in the Department of Computer Science, with a boss that has an unbelievable wealth of information. And this is where I am now.
